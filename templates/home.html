Our Team

Shalini Indrakanti Prasad Writer, Doctor, Outdoor-enthusiast

Shalini loves books and kids. The daughter of a special education teacher, Shalini grew up working with children on the autism spectrum in Northern California. She went to college at UC Berkeley, where she studied bionegineering and started a non-profit organization to spread health awareness amongst children. She then pursued medicine at Washington University in St. Louis. She is currently training to be a pediatric radiologist and pursuing neuroradiology research in autism. Shalini trained in Hindustani and Carnatic vocal music for the majority of her childhood. In her spare time, she enjoys gardening and pottery.

Ananya Arikarevala Artist, Photographer, Lover of books

Ananya was born and brought up in New Jersey. She is currently studying Communications Design at Pratt Institute. During her time at university, Ananya gained extensive training in the digital arts, affording her the opportunity to work for the renowned Vanity Fair and Vogue photographer, Annie Leibowitz. Exposed to Indian fine arts from a young age, Ananya has learnt and performed Bharthanatyam for over 10 years. She became involved with Parampara to share her Indian culture and traditions with the younger generation. When she is not drawing or dancing, she can be found curled up with a great read.

Our Story

Launching
Parampara

We grew up in America and love our culture. After growing reading Amar Chitra Kathas and Tinkle comics, we always felt the best stories were the ones we heard from our grandparents while laying in bed.

These stories usually started out with "A long long time ago, one day there lived a king...", and we were hooked. The stories that were passed down to us in this way taught us the values and heritage that hold our culture together in a new context and a different time. They also instilled in us timeless values that we aspire to in our daily lives.

We started Parampara to making learning about culture fun and engaging for children in a contextually relevant way. We would talk about common shared experiences: like our festivals, food, and common activities.

Shalini chose to bring together beautifully illustrated children's books (courtesy of Ameya's crazy skills) with activity kits that can engage children and help them fall in love with the characters that make up our folklore.
