Our Story

Launching
Parampara

We grew up in America and love our culture. Despite reading Amar Chitra Kathas and Tinkle comics, we always felt the best stories were the ones we heard from our grandparents during bedtime.

These stories usually started out with "A long long time ago, in a forest, there lived a king...", and we were hooked. Passed down in this way, we imbibed the heritage that holds our culture together in a new context and different time. They also instilled timeless values that we aspire to in our daily lives.

We started Parampara to make cultural learning fun, engaging, and contextually relevant for children of the Indian diaspora. Parampara creates toys, books, and activity kits to celebrate major Indian festivals, food and culture.

Our first culture-kit launches during Dasara. As part of this kit, there is an kid-friendly story book that explains the story of Dasara and how it is celebrated in different sub-cultures. We also include a coloring-book to reinforce the concepts covered in the book. Finally, we include a custom plushy of Goddess Durga and puja material so a small child can engage with the festival during all 10 days! There is even a prasadam tray so your little ones can offer their favorite candy-prasadam

The creators of Parampara are professionals in tech, medicine, and the arts. We are brought together by a shared love for celebrating our cultural heritage. We are so excited about Parampara and hope to share our passion with you.

Our Team

Shalini Indrakanti Prasad Writer, Doctor, Outdoor-enthusiast

Shalini loves books and kids. The daughter of a special education teacher, Shalini grew up working with children on the autism spectrum in Northern California. She went to college at UC Berkeley, where she studied bionegineering and started a non-profit organization to spread health awareness amongst children. She then pursued medicine at Washington University in St. Louis. She is currently training to be a pediatric radiologist and pursuing neuroradiology research in autism. Shalini trained in Hindustani and Carnatic vocal music for the majority of her childhood. In her spare time, she enjoys gardening and pottery.

Ananya Arikarevala Artist, Photographer, Lover of books

Ananya was born and brought up in New Jersey. She is currently studying Communications Design at Pratt Institute. During her time at university, Ananya gained extensive training in the digital arts, affording her the opportunity to work for the renowned Vanity Fair and Vogue photographer, Annie Leibowitz. Exposed to Indian fine arts from a young age, Ananya has learnt and performed Bharthanatyam for over 10 years. She became involved with Parampara to share her Indian culture and traditions with the younger generation. When she is not drawing or dancing, she can be found curled up with a great read.

Srikar Kasinadhuni Maker, Coder, Popsicle enthusiast

Srikar grew up the youngest of four brothers, and has held onto his childlike enthusiasm. He completed his undergraduate education at the University of Pennsylvania, where he started Penn Sargam, the first collegiate Indian classical music group in the United States. He also completed his MBA from the Yale School of Management. Srikar is a Veena artist, passionate woodworker, enthusiastic popsicle maker, and doting uncle.

Shyam Indrakanti Fixer, Dentist, Lover of gadgets

Shyam is a born teacher and forged his love for Indian culture during summers spent with his grandparents. While in high school in Norther California, and during his weekends while studying at UC Berkeley, Shyam volunteered at Bala Vihar and became a local life-coach to the many young students learning to balance their cultural identity in their daily American context. Shyam completed his dental degree at UCLA and is currently doing his residency in Oral and Maxilofacial Sugery in Dallas, Texas. When Shyam is not working or studying, he loves to tinker with electronics and curate his youtube channel, where he covers topics from "How to make headphones" to "How to build a home gym on a budget".
